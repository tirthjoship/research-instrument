What are my ~3 bets, and how do I raise it?▾

"3.2 effective bets" means: if you redrew your 66 holdings as a handful of uncorrelated wagers, you'd have about three. Names aren't bets — independent risks are. Here's what the three actually are:

BET 1 · 64% of your risk
Big-tech market beta

NVDA, MSFT, AAPL, GOOGL, AMZN, AMD… all load the same way. This is the one bet "concentration" keeps flagging. Selling one tech name to buy another does NOT reduce it.

BET 2 · 14%
Long-duration growth vs rates

A secondary tilt: your growth names rise when rates fall. Small, but a genuinely different axis from pure market direction.

BET 3 · 9%
Semis vs software dispersion

A thin idiosyncratic spread inside tech (chips behaving differently from software). The only place your stock-picking actually shows up.

How to react — raise effective bets
↑
Add exposure on an axis you're empty on. You have ~zero defensives, rates, commodities, or non-US. Adding any one loads a NEW principal portfolio → ENB rises (illustratively ~3.2 → ~3.9 with a 5% defensive sleeve).
✗
Don't reshuffle within Bet 1. Trading tech-for-tech leaves ENB flat — it's the same bet wearing a different ticker.
